How to do your abstract

If you have decided to take your courage in both hands and prepare an astounding abstract, here’s what you utterly need to know.

Both the paper and the abstract must be in English. The maximum number of words in an abstract is 350, without taking into consideration the following fields: Title, Author, Co-authors, Scientific Coordinators, Affiliation and Key-words.

The Title should not be longer than 12 words. Be creative and contain all the incredible ideas presented in the abstract in an alluring manner that will encourage everyone to read your valuable work.

WHO IS SUBMITTING THE ABSTRACT?

Each paper can have a maximum of 1 author, 3 co-authors and 6 scientific coordinators. The abstract is only going to be submitted by the author, who will also be the presenter of the paper. It is mandatory that the author has an active account and all co-authors have an active/not attending account, otherwise their registration will not be valid.

One ACTIVE PARTICIPANT can find himself only in one of the four following situations:

1) Author of a paper

2) Co-author of a paper

3) Author of a paper and Co-author of another paper

4) Co-author of two different papers

STRUCTURE FOR ORIGINAL STUDY

a) Background: It stands as a hipothesis for your study.

b) Materials and Methods : Relevant details about how the data was collected and processed and how it might varry.

c) Results : Present the main outcome of your original study in an attractive manner. Create a strong link between the original hypothesis and the final results, relating all of the ultimate data to it.

d) Conclusion : This section allows you to summarize the topic and make an objective comparison with the updated specialty literature. We suggest collecting further pieces of information from: Pub Med, Google score, Scopus preview etc. You can also add a powerful statement that comprehends the entire idea of the Original Study, allowing new perspectives over your abstract.

KEY-WORDS are mandatory, as they allow you to highlight the most important aspects of your abstract. Use them wisley, in order to comprehend the main ideas of your paper and to create a useful guide-line for the readers.
