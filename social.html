When a building can be seen from space, you know it's bound to be a statement up close. Get fast-track entrance and a guided tour of Bucharest's Palace of the Parliament and glimpse the extravagant architectural ambition and neoclassical style evident in every square inch of the world's second-largest administrative building.

Larger than the Great Pyramid of Giza, and second only to the Pentagon as the largest administrative building in the world, Bucharest's Palace of the Parliament stands as one of the most impressive buildings in the world - with a scarcely believable history to back it up!

Recommendations

The 'Victor Babeș' Museum

Open in GoogleMaps

Victor Babeş is one of the most renowned pioneers of the Romanian medical science of the 19th century, with significant contributions to the field of bacteriology and involvement in research concerning diseases such as rabies, leprosy, diphtheria and tuberculosis. As founder of the National Pathology and Bacteriology Institute and a teacher of pathological anatomy at the University of Bucharest, he was described by Gheorghe Marinescu, one of his students who later became a famed neurologist, as the most illustrious representative or Romanian medical science.

The museum that bears his name today was founded by his son, Mircea Babeş, in memory of the scientist’s great contributions to the world of medicine. Mircea gathered his father’s belongings from the apartment that Victor Babeş used in the National Pathology and Bacteriology Institute and , with them, created an exhibition on the ground floor of a house that he donated to the Bucharest City Hall. The house itself was designed in the eclectic style of the 1920s and built in the northern part of the city, in a quiet neighbourhood, initially in order to be used as a private residence, but today housing a collection consisting mostly of Victor Babeş's scientific work.

“Grigore Antipa” National Museum of Natural History

http://antipa.ro/en

“Grigore Antipa” National Museum of Natural History is one of the oldest research institutions in the field of biodiversity and public education, whilst simultaneously being one of the most praised and well known “databases” due to the Museum’s collections, some extremely valuable pieces of history.

The National History Museum of Romania

http://mnir.ro/

The National History Museum of Romania, founded in 1972, stands as one of the emblematic institutions of Romanian culture. Housed in a historic building formerly known as “The Post Palace”, the museum is located in the old city center of Bucharest. The building was designed by architect Alexandru Săvulescu (1847-1902), who drew inspiration from the Federal Post Palace in Geneva, and later erected between 1894 and 1899.

National Museum of Art of Romania

http://www.mnar.arts.ro/en/

The National Museum of Art of Romania is the country’s prime holder of Romanian, European and Oriental art. Located in the former Royal Palace in Bucharest, it includes both the National Gallery (Romanian medieval and modern art) and the European Art Gallery. Apart from numerous temporary exhibitions, visitors can also join guided tours of the former Throne Hall and other spaces of historical relevance.

Cotroceni National Museum

http://www.muzeulcotroceni.ro/index_eng.html

The Cotroceni National Museum is housed in the old Royal Palace, built in 1895, which stands apart as an truly unique site, due to the intertwinement between a location of great historical significance, with a history spanning 350 years, and an art museum. The focus of the museum is not the value of its various artefacts, but rather the connection its halls and apartments have had with the people who have lived there over the centuries.
