Recommendations

The Arch of Triumph

It is a monument built between 1921-1922 that celebrates the victory of Romania from The First World War. It has a height of 27 meters and on its façade you can read a lot of architectural elements that reminds us about the World War, a message from the King Ferdinand and the places where the battles were held. This monument filled with history will impress you with its history and greatness.

“Grigore Antipa” National Museum of Natural History

“Grigore Antipa” National Museum of Natural History is one of the oldest research institutions in the field of biodiversity and public education, whilst simultaneously being one of the most praised and well known “databases” due to the Museum’s collections, some extremely valuable pieces of history.

The National History Museum of Romania

The National History Museum of Romania, founded in 1972, stands as one of the emblematic institutions of Romanian culture. Housed in a historic building formerly known as “The Post Palace”, the museum is located in the old city center of Bucharest. The building was designed by architect Alexandru Săvulescu (1847-1902), who drew inspiration from the Federal Post Palace in Geneva, and later erected between 1894 and 1899.

National Museum of Art of Romania

The National Museum of Art of Romania is the country’s prime holder of Romanian, European and Oriental art. Located in the former Royal Palace in Bucharest, it includes both the National Gallery (Romanian medieval and modern art) and the European Art Gallery. Apart from numerous temporary exhibitions, visitors can also join guided tours of the former Throne Hall and other spaces of historical relevance.

Cotroceni National Museum

The Cotroceni National Museum is housed in the old Royal Palace, built in 1895, which stands apart as an truly unique site, due to the intertwinement between a location of great historical significance, with a history spanning 350 years, and an art museum. The focus of the museum is not the value of its various artefacts, but rather the connection its halls and apartments have had with the people who have lived there over the centuries.

The Palace of Parliament- “People’s House”

Friday, 6th of December, 10.00-12.00

Known as the “People’s House” before the Revolution, it has a height of 82 meters above ground and 92 meters underground. It is the second largest and heaviest administrative building in the world as mentioned by the Worlds Record Academy. In 1983, 700 architects started working to build this emblematic building that today can be visited by everyone who wants to see a part of our history.
